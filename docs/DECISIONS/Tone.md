Tone & Voice Manual: Leaf ID
1. Brand Personality
• Voice: Sophisticated, poetic, and scientific yet accessible.
• Inspiration: A mix of a National Geographic explorer’s field guide and a high-end travel journal (Airbnb/Apple style).
• Goal: Transform a simple photo-taking act into a "Botanical Discovery."
2. Terminology & Taxonomy
Whenever you (Cursor) generate UI text, labels, or placeholders, use the following naming conventions:
A. The Collection (User Gallery)
• Primary Name: The Herbarium
• Alternative (for UI elements): Folio or Leaf Deck.
• Avoid: "My Photos", "Gallery", "Library".
B. The Scanner (Camera/AI)
• Primary Name: Identify
• Process Name: Botanical Scan or Field Identification.
• AI Persona: Botanist AI.
• Avoid: "Scan", "Camera", "Check".
C. The Map (Geographical View)
• Primary Name: The Arboretum
• Concept: A map of where your discoveries "took root."
• Avoid: "Map", "Locations", "Places".
D. The Encyclopedia (Data/History)
• Primary Name: Tree Lore
• Data focus: Specimen Data or Origins.
• Avoid: "Info", "Details", "About", "Encyclopedia".
3. User & Social Identity (The "Mystical" Touch)
• User Profile: The Druid (represents the user’s growth and connection to nature).
• Achievements/Badges: Ancient Seeds.
• Actions: Use "Collect" or "Preserve" instead of "Save". Use "Gift a Discovery" instead of "Share".

5. Writing Principles for UI
1. Be Concise: Use powerful nouns (e.g., "Origin" instead of "This tree comes from...").
2. Evoke Emotion: Instead of "Loading...", use "Consulting the Lore..." or "Analyzing Specimen...".
3. The "Apple" Rule: If it sounds too technical, simplify it. If it sounds too boring, make it "Botanical".
